Overview

Purpose

I’m a Montessori Elementary teacher. I’m a computer programmer. I make mobile apps that allow students, parents, and teachers to stay connected, whether they are in the classroom or exploring their world. More importantly I make apps that are engaging and easy to use. Think about the last time your child was “doing homeschool.” Did everything go smoothly? That’s what I do. . . I make apps that engage kids at their level and are so intuitive that parents/teachers do not need instructions to use it. We know the kids won’t need instructions; they can figure out any app ;

Audience

Our target audience is made up of students and families that are working from home due to illness or vacation. This audience also includes teachers who are required to provide assignments and lessons to these students. These teachers have a minimum amount of time to plan, gather, and organize the materials needed for the at home activities. The students and families are not super eager to complete work while they are sick (totally understandable) and who wants to do school work while they are on vacation? Access will be through laptop, desktop, and mobile devices.

Welcome (Home page)

Welcome to your classroom portal and the 2022/23 school year. My name is Mrs. Astons and I am super excited that we will be learning and exploring together. This website will help us stay connected when you are at home or on the go. You will find:

  • videos and activities
  • Montessori math
  • silly stories
  • exciting adventures
  • suspencful mysteries
  • step-by-step writing opportunities
  • helpful spelling games

A Note to Parents

Dear Parents, This classroom portal is designed to give you a glimpse into the daily classroom activities and to make connections with at home learning. Activities are based on the multiple learning modalities of your little scholar. You are the primary educator. All activities and lessons are suggestions. You are welcome to change, add, or twist any and all assignments. If you have any questions, please do not hesitate to ask. It is a privilege to be your partner in education.

You May Need:

These are a few things that you may need as you work on your activities this week:

  • Pencil
  • Eraser
  • Journal or Lined Paper
  • Crayons
  • Scissors
  • Glue Stick

Let's Get Started

You are ready to begin. Check out the tabs at the top of the page. Click on your grade. You will find an online 'work-plan.' Look around. Take a minute to see what we are exploring this week. Choose an activity to start your day. Take a break from time to time. Most of all...

Have FUN!

Classroom Adventures

Super Seconds (page 3)

Classroom Adventures

Ancient Greece was an interesting place to live. We learned that they believed in many gods and that each god had a special power or responsibility. We know that Ancient Greece was in Europe and was much larger than present day Greece. We know that present day Greece is on a peninsula in Europe and has many islands. We created beautiful topographical maps of Europe. We then stretched our own creativity by designing and painting a topographical map of an island that we imagined.

Third Grade Leaders (page 4)

Classroom Adventures

WOW! We are becoming responsible and compassionate leaders. We are examples to our classmates of curious, self-guided learners. We have fallen in love with Ancient Roman architecture. We are using arches and columns in designing and building models of ancient colliseums, homes, and temples. Some have included a few modern features that they feel are essential then and now. Some are adding 'people' to show the different social classes: patricians, plebeians, and slaves. We are learning that the ancient Romans experimented with democracy, allowing regular people to make laws rather than kings, emperors, pharaohs, etc.
